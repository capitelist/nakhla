# Moderator Playbook

Guide for adult moderators facilitating Nakhla exchange sessions.

---

## Your Role

You are a **facilitator**, not a teacher. Your job is to:
- Keep conversations flowing in breakout pods
- Ensure every child gets a turn to speak
- Handle any issues calmly and quickly
- Make it fun

You are NOT expected to:
- Teach content (teachers handle the full-group blocks)
- Translate in real-time (kids use English as bridge + learned phrases)
- Resolve deep conflicts (escalate to lead teacher)

---

## Pre-Session Checklist

### 1 Week Before
- [ ] Confirm your session date and time
- [ ] Review the session content pack (vocab, prompts, activity)
- [ ] Test Google Meet link — can you join? Can you access breakout rooms?
- [ ] Review pod assignments (which kids are in your pods)
- [ ] Read the escalation protocol below

### 1 Day Before
- [ ] Review discussion prompts for this session
- [ ] Prepare your screen: Google Meet open, prompts visible on second screen/printout
- [ ] Test your microphone and camera
- [ ] Charge your device

### 15 Minutes Before Session
- [ ] Join Google Meet early
- [ ] Confirm with lead teacher: pod assignments, breakout room numbers
- [ ] Have escalation contact (lead teacher) phone number ready
- [ ] Open the discussion prompts document

### During Session
- [ ] Full Group (0–10 min): Camera on, mic muted, model attentive listening
- [ ] Pod Pairs (10–35 min): Float between your assigned breakout rooms
- [ ] Activity (35–50 min): Help kids navigate Kahoot/Jamboard if needed
- [ ] Wrap-Up (50–60 min): Camera on, encourage kids to share

---

## Breakout Room Management (Google Meet)

### How Breakout Rooms Work
- Lead teacher creates and assigns rooms before opening them
- You are assigned to float between 2–3 rooms
- You can enter/leave rooms freely during the pod block

### Rotation Protocol
1. Start in your first assigned room — stay 8 minutes
2. Move to second room — stay 8 minutes
3. Move to third room (if assigned) — stay 8 minutes
4. Return to first room for final check-in

### What to Do in Each Room
1. **Arrive:** "Hi everyone! How's it going? What are you talking about?"
2. **Listen:** Let kids lead. Only intervene if conversation stalls.
3. **Prompt:** If quiet, read the next discussion prompt from the list.
4. **Encourage:** "That's really interesting! [Name], what do you think?"
5. **Exit:** "Great job! Keep going — I'll be back to check on you."

## Facilitation Phrases

### Use These (Encouraging, Open)
| English | Hebrew | Arabic |
|---------|--------|--------|
| "That's really interesting!" | "!זה ממש מעניין" | "!هذا مثير للاهتمام حقاً" |
| "Tell me more about that" | "ספר/י לי עוד על זה" | "أخبرني المزيد عن ذلك" |
| "Does anyone else want to share?" | "?מישהו עוד רוצה לשתף" | "هل يريد أحد آخر أن يشارك؟" |
| "Great job everyone!" | "!כל הכבוד לכולם" | "!أحسنتم جميعاً" |
| "Let's hear from [name]" | "[שם] בואו נשמע מ" | "دعونا نسمع من [اسم]" |
| "It's okay to be shy" | "זה בסדר להיות ביישן/ית" | "لا بأس أن تكون خجولاً" |
| "You don't have to share if you don't want to" | "לא חייבים לשתף אם לא רוצים" | "لا يجب عليك المشاركة إذا لم ترغب" |

### Avoid These
| Don't Say | Why | Say Instead |
|-----------|-----|-------------|
| "In your country, do you…?" | Implies otherness | "Where you live, what's it like?" |
| "That's wrong" | Shuts kids down | "Interesting — tell me why you think that" |
| "You should know this" | Pressure | "No worries — let's learn it together" |
| "Who's winning?" | Competitive framing | "What did we learn?" |
| "Are things safe where you live?" | Conflict reference | (Don't ask this at all) |
| "Do you like [other country]?" | Political framing | "What's something cool you learned today?" |
| "Is it true that in [country]…" | Stereotyping | "Tell us about YOUR experience" |
| "You're so brave for doing this" | Implies danger/difficulty | "I'm glad you're here!" |

## Escalation Protocol

### Scenario 1: Bullying or Mean Behavior
**Signs:** Name-calling, mocking accent/language, exclusion, laughing at someone.

**Response:**
1. Calmly intervene: "Let's pause for a moment. In Nakhla, we treat each other with respect."
2. Redirect: "Let's move to the next question."
3. If it continues: Remove the child from breakout → return them to main room.
4. Message lead teacher immediately (Google Meet chat or phone).
5. Lead teacher contacts the child's home teacher and parents.
6. Document the incident after the session.

**Do NOT:** Shame the child publicly, ignore it, or try to mediate a conflict between children.

### Scenario 2: Child in Distress (Crying, Upset, Withdrawn)
**Signs:** Tears, silence, turning camera off suddenly, saying "I want to leave."

**Response:**
1. Gently: "It's okay. You can take a break. You don't have to talk right now."
2. If the child wants to leave: Move them to main room (safe space with lead teacher).
3. Do NOT pressure them to explain why they're upset.
4. Message lead teacher immediately.
5. Lead teacher contacts parents if needed.

**Do NOT:** Ask "What's wrong?" repeatedly, draw attention to the child, or prevent them from leaving.

### Scenario 3: Technical Failure
**Signs:** Audio/video not working, child can't join breakout, screen freeze.

**Response:**
1. Ask the child to leave and rejoin the Meet.
2. If breakout room fails: Use Google Meet chat for text-based conversation.
3. If a child can't rejoin: Message lead teacher to help troubleshoot.
4. If Meet itself goes down: Lead teacher sends WhatsApp message to all parents with backup plan.

**Backup plan:** Continue via WhatsApp voice note exchange (record prompts, send to pods).

### Scenario 4: Off-Topic or Inappropriate Content
**Signs:** Children discussing politics, violence, religion in a charged way, sharing inappropriate images.

**Response:**
1. Redirect immediately: "That's an interesting topic, but today we're talking about [session theme]."
2. If it's about conflict/politics specifically: "We're here to learn about each other's daily lives — let's focus on that."
3. If inappropriate content is shared on screen: "Let's keep our sharing to [today's activity]. Can you show us your [Jamboard/drawing] instead?"
4. Report to lead teacher after session.

**Do NOT:** Engage with political topics, share your own views, or allow debate on sensitive issues.

### Scenario 5: Language Barrier (Child Can't Communicate)
**Signs:** Child is silent because they don't understand, not because they're shy.

**Response:**
1. Switch to simpler English: short sentences, basic words.
2. Use visual aids: thumbs up/down, show pictures, use Jamboard to draw.
3. Ask their pod partners to help: "Can someone help explain?"
4. Use the vocab sheet: point to words, have them repeat.
5. If persistent: Message lead teacher — they may arrange a same-language buddy.

**Do NOT:** Speak louder (they can hear you fine), skip them, or make them feel bad about language skills.
