# Nakhla — Virtual Exchange Program

**3 sessions | 60 minutes each | Ages 8–12 | Trilingual (Hebrew / Arabic / English)**

Nakhla (نخلة / נַחְלָה) means "date palm" — a tree shared across both cultures, rooted in the same soil.

---

## Program Arc

| Session | Title | Theme | Goal |
|---------|-------|-------|------|
| 1 | Hello, Neighbor | Greetings & introductions | Learn each other's names, basic phrases, where we live |
| 2 | Our World | Food, family, music, games | Share daily life and discover similarities |
| 3 | Friends Across the Shore | Live conversation & art | Build genuine connection, say goodbye with a shared creation |

---

## Session Format (All Sessions)

Every session follows the same 60-minute structure:

| Time | Block | Format | Description |
|------|-------|--------|-------------|
| 00:00–10:00 | **Full Group** | Teacher-led, Google Meet main room | Welcome, recap, introduce today's theme |
| 10:00–35:00 | **Pod Pairs** | Breakout rooms (2 Israeli + 2 Emirati kids) | Guided conversation using discussion prompts |
| 35:00–50:00 | **Activity** | Kahoot or Jamboard (full group) | Collaborative quiz or shared creative work |
| 50:00–60:00 | **Wrap-Up** | Full group, teacher-led | Reflections, shout-outs, preview next session |

### Pod Pair Rules
- Each pod = 2 Israeli children + 2 Emirati children
- One adult moderator per 2–3 pods (floating)
- English is the bridge language; Hebrew and Arabic are encouraged for greetings/phrases
- Moderator rotates pods between sessions so kids meet new partners

## Session 1: Hello, Neighbor

**Theme:** Greetings, names, where we live, basic language intro

### Full Group (10 min)
- Teacher introduces the program: "We're going to meet kids who live across the sea"
- Show a simple map — Israel and UAE, the shared Gulf coast
- Teach 3 words everyone will use today: Hello (שלום / مرحبا / Hello), My name is… (…שמי / …اسمي / My name is…), Thank you (תודה / شكراً / Thank you)

### Pod Pairs (25 min)
Use `discussion-prompts.md` — structured questions:
1. Say hello in your language, teach your pod partners
2. What's your name? What does it mean?
3. What city/town do you live in? Describe one thing you see from your window
4. What's your favorite thing to do after school?
5. Teach your partners one word in your language

### Activity (15 min)
- **Kahoot Quiz:** 10 trilingual questions about greetings and basic facts (see `kahoot-questions.json`)
- No visible scoring — Kahoot set to "no podium" mode
- Questions appear in English; answer options include Hebrew and Arabic

### Wrap-Up (10 min)
- Each kid shares one new word they learned
- Teacher previews Session 2: "Next time, we'll share our favorite foods and music!"
- **Parent tip** distributed via WhatsApp group

## Session 2: Our World

**Theme:** Food, family, music, games — sharing daily life

### Full Group (10 min)
- Recap: "Last time we learned each other's names and greetings"
- Quick review: everyone says hello in all 3 languages
- Introduce today's theme: "What does your world look like? What do you eat, listen to, play?"

### Pod Pairs (25 min)
Use `discussion-prompts.md`:
1. What's your favorite food? Describe it — what does it taste like?
2. How many people are in your family? Do you have pets?
3. What music do you listen to? Can you hum or sing a little bit?
4. What games do you play with friends?
5. What's one thing you think is special about where you live?

### Activity (15 min)
- **Collaborative Jamboard:** Each pod creates a shared board with drawings/images
- Board divided into 4 squares: My Favorite Food / My Family / My Music / My Games
- Each kid fills one square with a drawing or image

### Wrap-Up (10 min)
- Pods share their Jamboard with the full group (screen share)
- Teacher highlights similarities: "Look — both groups love rice dishes! Both groups play football!"
- Preview Session 3: "Next time is our last meeting — we'll create something together"

## Session 3: Friends Across the Shore

**Theme:** Live conversation, shared creation, friendship ceremony

### Full Group (10 min)
- Recap: "We've learned greetings and shared our worlds — today we celebrate our new friendships"
- Introduce the session: free conversation + a shared art project + goodbye ceremony

### Pod Pairs (25 min)
Use `discussion-prompts.md`:
1. What was your favorite moment from our sessions?
2. If your pod partner visited your city, where would you take them?
3. What's one thing you learned about each other's culture that surprised you?
4. If we could do one more activity together, what would it be?
5. What message would you like to send to your new friends?

**Shared Art Activity (within pod time):**
- Each pod creates a "Friendship Postcard" on Jamboard
- One side: draw something from YOUR culture
- Other side: draw something from your PARTNER'S culture
- Write a message in all 3 languages

### Activity (15 min)
- **Kahoot Quiz:** Fun review of everything learned across all 3 sessions
- Pods share their Friendship Postcards with the full group

### Wrap-Up / Goodbye Ceremony (10 min)
- Teacher reads out each child's name and presents a **Friendship Certificate** (digital, printable)
- Each child says goodbye in all 3 languages: שלום / مع السلامة / Goodbye
- Teacher: "The palm tree grows slowly, but its roots go deep. You've planted something today."
- Information shared about how to stay connected (Google Sites portfolio)

## Guiding Principles

1. **No politics.** No conflict references. Pure cultural curiosity.
2. **Trauma-aware.** No sudden loud sounds, no competitive scoring visible to children, no pressure to share more than they want.
3. **Trilingual always.** Every piece of content exists in Hebrew, Arabic, and English.
4. **Agency.** Children choose what to share. Nothing is mandatory.
5. **Joy first.** If it's not fun, it's not working.
