# Blooket Setup — Session 1: Hello, Neighbor!

Step-by-step to create the Blooket game from `kahoot-questions.json`.

---

## Create the Question Set

1. Go to **blooket.com** and log in to your teacher account
2. Click **Create** → **Create a Set**
3. **Title:** "Nakhla Session 1: Hello, Neighbor!"
4. **Description:** "Trilingual quiz about greetings and introductions"
5. Add each question from `kahoot-questions.json`:

### Questions to Add

| # | Question | Correct Answer |
|---|----------|---------------|
| 1 | How do you say 'Hello' in Hebrew? | שָׁלוֹם (Shalom) |
| 2 | How do you say 'Hello' in Arabic? | مَرْحَبًا (Marhaba) |
| 3 | What does 'Toda' (תּוֹדָה) mean in English? | Thank you |
| 4 | What does 'Shukran' (شُكْرًا) mean in English? | Thank you |
| 5 | How do you say 'My name is...' in Hebrew? | ...שְׁמִי (Shmi...) |
| 6 | How do you say 'Friend' in Arabic? | صَدِيق (Sadiq) |
| 7 | What does 'Na'im me'od' (נָעִים מְאוֹד) mean? | Nice to meet you |
| 8 | How do you say 'Goodbye' in Arabic? | مَعَ السَّلَامَة (Ma'a as-salama) |
| 9 | Which pair both mean 'Yes'? | כֵּן (Ken) + نَعَم (Na'am) |
| 10 | What do 'Shalom' and 'Ma'a as-salama' have in common? | Both come from a root meaning 'peace' |

6. For each question: enter the question text, add 4 answer options, and select the correct one
7. **Time limit:** 30 seconds per question (set in game settings when hosting)
8. Click **Save**

---

## Host the Game

1. From your Blooket dashboard, find the Question Set
2. Click **Host**
3. Choose game mode: **Gold Quest** (recommended — collaborative feel, less competitive)
4. Students go to **play.blooket.com** and enter the Game ID shown on your screen
5. Students enter a nickname (first name only) — no account needed
6. Share your screen via Zoom so everyone sees the game
7. After each question, read the correct answer aloud in all 3 languages

---

## Tips
- **Gold Quest** mode is best for Nakhla — avoids elimination and intense competition
- If students struggle with reading Hebrew/Arabic script, read the options aloud
- Celebrate participation, not scores — "Great job everyone!" not "Who got the most right?"
- Free tier: unlimited games, up to 60 players
